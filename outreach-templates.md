## Before You Start: Outreach Best Practices

**Personalize every email.** Mention the recipient's name, their site, a specific article they wrote, or something you genuinely liked. Generic emails get deleted.

**Research first, email second.** Spend 2-3 minutes on their site before writing. Reference something specific. This is the single biggest factor in getting replies.

**Follow up exactly once.** Send one follow-up 5-7 days after the initial email. If they don't respond after that, move on. Never send a third email.

**Expected response rates:** 5-15% is normal for cold outreach. Don't get discouraged. If you send 15 emails per week, expect 1-2 replies. That adds up fast over months.

**Weekly volume:** Aim for 10-20 outreach emails per week. More than that and quality drops. Less than that and you won't build momentum.

**Track everything.** Use a simple spreadsheet: recipient name, site, date sent, follow-up date, response, outcome.

## Template 1: Directory Listing Introduction

**When to use:** Submitting to agency directories like GoodFirms, DesignRush, Digital Agency Network, Clutch, or UpCity.

### Subject Line
Listing request — Ottawa web design agency

### Email Body

Hi [NAME],

I run Neon Web Agency, a web design studio based in Ottawa, Ontario. We specialize in building fast, SEO-optimized websites for small businesses and service providers in the Ottawa area.

I'd like to submit our agency for listing on [DIRECTORY NAME]. I noticed you feature agencies across Canada and thought we'd be a good fit for your [SPECIFIC CATEGORY, e.g., "Canadian web design agencies" or "top agencies in Ontario"] section.

Here's our site: https://neoncraft.me

Happy to provide any additional details you need for the listing. What's the best way to get started?

Thanks,
[YOUR NAME]
Neon Web Agency

### Follow-Up (5-7 days later)

Subject: Re: Listing request — Ottawa web design agency

Hi [NAME], just circling back on this. If there's an application form or specific requirements for getting listed, I'm happy to fill those out. Let me know!

### Customization Tips
- Some directories have a self-service submission form. Use the email only when there's no obvious form, or when the form requires approval.
- Mention a specific category or page on their directory where you'd fit.
- If they charge for premium listings, decide your budget beforehand.

## Template 2: Local Business Blog Guest Post Pitch

**When to use:** Pitching guest articles to Ottawa Business Journal, Apt613, local tech blogs, Chamber of Commerce blogs, or any Ottawa-focused publication that accepts contributors.

### Subject Line
Guest post idea: [SPECIFIC TOPIC] for [PUBLICATION NAME] readers

### Email Body

Hi [NAME],

I've been reading [PUBLICATION NAME] for a while — I especially liked your recent piece on [SPECIFIC ARTICLE OR TOPIC]. Good stuff.

I'd love to contribute a guest article for your readers. I had a couple of ideas:

- [TOPIC IDEA 1, e.g., "5 things Ottawa small businesses get wrong about their websites"]
- [TOPIC IDEA 2, e.g., "Why page speed matters more than design for local businesses"]

I run a web design agency here in Ottawa, so I can speak to this from hands-on experience working with local businesses. I'd keep it practical and useful — no fluff, no sales pitch.

Would either of those work for your audience? Open to other angles too.

Thanks,
[YOUR NAME]
Neon Web Agency | neoncraft.me

### Follow-Up (5-7 days later)

Subject: Re: Guest post idea for [PUBLICATION NAME] readers

Hi [NAME], just wanted to follow up on this. Completely understand if the timing isn't right — happy to revisit down the road if that works better. Either way, keep up the great work with [PUBLICATION NAME].

### Customization Tips
- Always pitch 2-3 specific topic ideas. Don't make them come up with the idea.
- Reference a real article from their site. This proves you actually read it.
- Offer topics relevant to their audience, not just topics that promote your business.
- If accepted, include one natural link to neoncraft.me within the article (not in the bio — in-content links carry more weight).

## Template 3: Resource Page Link Request

**When to use:** When you find pages that list Ottawa businesses, web design resources, small business tools, or "best of" resource compilations.

### Subject Line
Suggestion for your [PAGE TOPIC] page

### Email Body

Hi [NAME],

I came across your [PAGE TITLE, e.g., "Ottawa Small Business Resources"] page while researching [TOPIC]. Great list — I actually bookmarked a few of the links myself.

I noticed you include [TYPE OF RESOURCES, e.g., "local web and marketing services"]. We recently published a guide on [RELEVANT RESOURCE, e.g., "website speed optimization for small businesses"] that might be useful for your readers: [LINK TO YOUR RESOURCE].

If you think it fits, feel free to add it. Either way, thanks for putting that page together — it's genuinely helpful.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
Neon Web Agency

### Follow-Up (5-7 days later)

Subject: Re: Suggestion for your [PAGE TOPIC] page

Hi [NAME], just a quick follow-up on this. No pressure at all — just wanted to make sure it didn't get buried. Thanks again for the great resource page.

### Customization Tips
- This works best when you link to a genuinely useful resource (a blog post, guide, or tool) rather than your homepage.
- The blog posts on neoncraft.me/blog are good candidates to link to here.
- Be specific about which page you're referencing. Include the URL if helpful.
- Keep the tone casual. You're making a suggestion, not a demand.

## Template 4: Listicle Inclusion Request

**When to use:** When you find articles titled "Best Web Design Agencies in Ottawa," "Top Ottawa Digital Agencies," or similar roundups where your agency is not yet listed.

### Subject Line
Quick note about your Ottawa web design agencies article

### Email Body

Hi [NAME],

I'd love for Neon Web Agency to be considered for a future update. We're an Ottawa-based studio focused on fast, SEO-optimized websites for small businesses and service providers. A few quick highlights:

- Based in Ottawa, serving local businesses across [NEIGHBORHOODS, e.g., "Kanata, Barrhaven, Orleans, and downtown"]
- Specializing in Next.js and modern web performance
- [ONE SPECIFIC PROOF POINT, e.g., "Recently helped a local contractor increase organic traffic by 40%"]

Here's our site if you'd like to take a look: https://neoncraft.me

Thanks for the great content either way.

[YOUR NAME]
Neon Web Agency

### Follow-Up (5-7 days later)

Subject: Re: Quick note about your Ottawa web design agencies article

Hi [NAME], just following up briefly. If you're planning any updates to the list, I'd be happy to provide any additional info you might need. Thanks!

### Customization Tips
- These articles get updated periodically. Even if they don't add you now, they might in the next refresh.
- If the article ranks well on Google, a link from it is especially valuable.
- Include 2-3 bullet points that make you easy to write about. Save them the research.
- Never criticize agencies already on the list.

## Template 5: Local Partnership / Cross-Promotion

**When to use:** Reaching out to complementary Ottawa businesses — photographers, copywriters, marketing consultants, accountants, bookkeepers, branding agencies — who might refer clients to you (and vice versa).

### Subject Line
Referral idea — [YOUR SERVICE] + [THEIR SERVICE] in Ottawa

### Email Body

Hi [NAME],

I run Neon Web Agency here in Ottawa — we build websites for small businesses and service providers. I came across [THEIR BUSINESS NAME] and really liked [SPECIFIC THING, e.g., "your portfolio" or "the branding work you did for [CLIENT]"].

We regularly get asked by our clients for recommendations for [THEIR SERVICE, e.g., "professional photography" or "bookkeeping"]. I'd love to start referring people your way if you're open to it.

If it makes sense on your end, we'd appreciate being on your radar for clients who need a new website or a redesign. No formal agreement needed — just a mutual "hey, check these people out" kind of thing.

Would you be up for a quick coffee or a 15-minute call sometime?

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
Neon Web Agency | neoncraft.me

### Follow-Up (5-7 days later)

Subject: Re: Referral idea — [YOUR SERVICE] + [THEIR SERVICE] in Ottawa

Hi [NAME], just bumping this up in case it got lost in the inbox. Happy to work around your schedule if you're interested. If not, no worries at all.

### Customization Tips
- This is a relationship play, not a link-building play. The backlink happens naturally over time when they add you to their "partners" or "recommended" page.
- Lead with what you can do for them (send referrals), not what you want (a link).
- Actually follow through on referrals. This only works if it's genuine.
- Local Ottawa networking events (like Ottawa Chamber of Commerce mixers) are a great place to warm up these contacts before emailing.

## Template 6: Client Testimonial + Review Request

**When to use:** After completing a project for a client, or when checking in with a past client who was happy with your work. Targets Google Business Profile reviews and website testimonials.

### Subject Line
Quick favor — 30-second Google review?

### Email Body

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

Hope things are going well with [THEIR BUSINESS / THE PROJECT, e.g., "the new site" or "the website redesign"]. It was a great project to work on.

If you've had a positive experience working with us, would you mind leaving a quick Google review? It makes a huge difference for a small Ottawa business like ours. Most people keep it to 2-3 sentences — doesn't need to be long.

Here's the direct link: [GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]

And if you're open to it, I'd also love to feature a short testimonial from you on our website. Totally fine to say no — I appreciate the work we've done together either way.

Thanks, [CLIENT NAME]. Really glad we could help with [SPECIFIC DELIVERABLE].

[YOUR NAME]
Neon Web Agency

### Follow-Up (5-7 days later)

Subject: Re: Quick favor — 30-second Google review?

Hi [CLIENT NAME], just a gentle nudge on this. I know you're busy — if you have 30 seconds, a quick star rating and a sentence or two would mean the world. Here's that link again: [GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]. Thanks either way!

### Customization Tips
- Send this within 2-4 weeks of project completion, while the experience is still fresh.
- Make the Google review link as direct as possible (use the short URL from your Google Business Profile).
- Reference the specific project so it feels personal, not automated.
- If they leave a review, thank them personally. If they also provide a testimonial, ask permission before publishing it.
- Never incentivize reviews (no discounts or gifts in exchange). Google penalizes this.
