## Why Choosing the Right Web Designer Matters

Your website is the digital face of your business. In Ottawa's competitive market, it needs to look professional, load fast, rank on Google, and convert visitors into customers. The web designer you choose will directly impact whether your website achieves these goals or falls short.

A great web designer doesn't just make things look pretty — they understand business strategy, user psychology, SEO, and conversion optimization. The wrong choice can cost you thousands in wasted investment and months of lost revenue from an underperforming website.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

**Can I see your portfolio?** Review their recent work. Do the sites look modern and professional? Are they mobile-friendly? Do they load quickly? Visit the actual live sites, not just screenshots.

**Do you specialize in businesses like mine?** Experience in your industry matters. A designer who has built websites for [dental practices](/industries/dental/) understands patient trust signals. One experienced with [restaurants](/industries/restaurants/) knows about online ordering integration. Industry-specific expertise translates to better results.

**What's included in the price?** Get a detailed breakdown. Does the quote include SEO optimization, mobile-responsive design, content creation, and post-launch support? The [cost of a website](/blog/web-design-cost-ottawa/) varies widely based on what's included.

**Who will own the website?** This is critical. Some agencies retain ownership of your website or domain, making it expensive or difficult to leave. Ensure you own everything — domain, hosting account, and all website files.

**What CMS will you use?** Understanding whether they'll build on [WordPress, Shopify, or a custom platform](/blog/wordpress-vs-custom-website/) helps you plan for long-term maintenance and content management.

## Red Flags to Watch For

**No portfolio or only showing template demos.** Legitimate agencies proudly display their work. If they can't show real client websites, proceed with caution.

**Extremely low prices.** If someone offers a professional website for $300-$500, they're likely using a cheap template with minimal customization. You get what you pay for.

**No mention of SEO.** Any reputable web design agency in 2026 should include [SEO](/services/seo/) as a core part of their service. If SEO is an expensive add-on or afterthought, the agency may not understand modern web design.

**Long-term contracts with no exit clause.** Some agencies lock you into multi-year contracts. A confident agency doesn't need to trap clients — they retain them through great work.

**No clear process or timeline.** Professional agencies have a defined process: discovery, design, development, testing, launch. If they can't explain their process, they likely don't have one.
